BANKING FAQ - COMPREHENSIVE GUIDE
===================================

SECTION 1: HOME LOANS
----------------------

Q: What documents are required for a home loan?
A: The following documents are required for a home loan application:
- Identity Proof: Aadhaar Card, PAN Card, Passport, or Voter ID
- Address Proof: Utility bills, Aadhaar Card, or Passport
- Income Proof: Last 3 months salary slips, Form 16, last 2 years ITR
- Employment Proof: Employment letter or offer letter
- Property Documents: Sale deed, NOC from builder, property title deed
- Bank Statements: Last 6 months bank statements
- Passport-sized photographs: 2 recent photographs
- Credit Report: CIBIL score report (minimum score required: 700)

Q: What is the minimum CIBIL score required for a home loan?
A: The minimum CIBIL score required is 700. A score above 750 is considered excellent and may qualify for better interest rates.

Q: What is the maximum tenure for a home loan?
A: Home loans can be availed for a maximum tenure of 30 years. The tenure depends on the applicant's age at the time of loan maturity (typically not exceeding 70 years of age).

Q: What is the maximum loan amount for a home loan?
A: Home loans are available up to 90% of the property value for loans up to Rs. 30 lakhs, 80% for loans between Rs. 30 lakhs and Rs. 75 lakhs, and 75% for loans above Rs. 75 lakhs.

Q: What are the current home loan interest rates?
A: Home loan interest rates currently range from 8.40% to 9.85% per annum depending on the loan amount, tenure, and applicant profile. Floating rates are linked to the RBI repo rate.

SECTION 2: PERSONAL LOANS
--------------------------

Q: What is the processing fee for a personal loan?
A: The processing fee for a personal loan is 1% to 2% of the loan amount, subject to a minimum of Rs. 1,000 and a maximum of Rs. 15,000. GST is applicable on the processing fee at 18%.

Q: What documents are required for a personal loan?
A: Documents required for a personal loan:
- Identity Proof: PAN Card, Aadhaar Card, Passport
- Address Proof: Aadhaar Card, utility bills, rental agreement
- Income Proof: Last 3 months salary slips, last 6 months bank statements
- Employment Proof: Current employment letter or offer letter
- Form 16 or last 2 years ITR

Q: What is the maximum personal loan amount?
A: Personal loans are available up to Rs. 40 lakhs for salaried individuals. The loan amount is determined based on monthly income, existing EMIs, and credit score.

Q: What is the personal loan interest rate?
A: Personal loan interest rates range from 10.50% to 18.00% per annum. Rates depend on your income, credit score, employer category, and loan tenure.

Q: What is the maximum tenure for a personal loan?
A: Personal loans can be availed for a maximum tenure of 60 months (5 years).

SECTION 3: SAVINGS ACCOUNTS
-----------------------------

Q: How do I open a zero balance account?
A: You can open a zero balance account (Basic Savings Bank Deposit Account - BSBDA) by:
1. Visiting your nearest branch with identity and address proof
2. Applying online through our net banking portal
3. Using our mobile app with Video KYC
Required documents: Aadhaar Card and PAN Card (or Form 60 if no PAN)
Note: BSBDA accounts have a maximum balance limit of Rs. 50,000 and maximum credit in a year of Rs. 1,00,000.

Q: What is the minimum balance required in a regular savings account?
A: The minimum average quarterly balance (AQB) required is:
- Metro and Urban branches: Rs. 10,000
- Semi-Urban branches: Rs. 5,000
- Rural branches: Rs. 2,500
Non-maintenance charges: Rs. 100 to Rs. 750 per quarter depending on the shortfall.

Q: What is the interest rate on savings accounts?
A: Savings account interest rates are:
- Balance up to Rs. 1 lakh: 3.00% per annum
- Balance above Rs. 1 lakh: 3.50% per annum
Interest is calculated on daily closing balance and credited quarterly.

SECTION 4: FIXED DEPOSITS
--------------------------

Q: What are the current fixed deposit interest rates?
A: Fixed Deposit interest rates (per annum):
- 7 days to 29 days: 3.50%
- 30 days to 90 days: 4.50%
- 91 days to 180 days: 5.50%
- 181 days to less than 1 year: 6.00%
- 1 year to less than 2 years: 7.10%
- 2 years to less than 3 years: 7.00%
- 3 years to 5 years: 6.75%
- Above 5 years: 6.50%
Senior citizens get an additional 0.50% on all tenures.

Q: What is the minimum amount to open a fixed deposit?
A: The minimum amount to open a Fixed Deposit is Rs. 1,000. There is no maximum limit.

Q: Can I break my FD before maturity?
A: Yes, premature withdrawal of FD is allowed. A penalty of 1% is deducted from the applicable interest rate for premature closure. Liquid FDs (with no premature withdrawal penalty) are also available for select tenures.

SECTION 5: CREDIT CARDS
------------------------

Q: How do I apply for a credit card?
A: You can apply for a credit card through:
1. Online: Visit our website or mobile app, go to Cards > Credit Cards > Apply Now
2. Branch: Visit your nearest branch with required documents
3. Pre-approved: Existing customers may receive pre-approved credit card offers in net banking

Required documents:
- Identity Proof: PAN Card (mandatory), Aadhaar Card
- Address Proof: Aadhaar Card, utility bills
- Income Proof: Last 3 months salary slips or ITR for self-employed
- Recent photographs

Eligibility:
- Minimum age: 21 years (add-on card: 18 years)
- Minimum income: Rs. 25,000 per month (salaried) or Rs. 3 lakhs per annum (self-employed)
- Good credit score required (minimum 700)

Q: What is the credit limit on a new credit card?
A: The initial credit limit is set based on your income, credit history, and existing obligations. Typically ranges from Rs. 50,000 to Rs. 5,00,000. You can request a credit limit increase after 6 months of good repayment history.

Q: What are the charges for late payment on a credit card?
A: Late payment charges are:
- Outstanding up to Rs. 500: No charge
- Outstanding Rs. 501 to Rs. 10,000: Rs. 500
- Outstanding Rs. 10,001 to Rs. 25,000: Rs. 750
- Outstanding above Rs. 25,000: Rs. 950
Interest on outstanding amount: 3.49% per month (41.88% per annum)

SECTION 6: DIGITAL BANKING
----------------------------

Q: How do I register for net banking?
A: To register for net banking:
1. Visit our website and click on "New User Registration"
2. Enter your account number and registered mobile number
3. Verify with OTP sent to your registered mobile
4. Set your login ID and password
5. Complete second-factor authentication setup
Alternatively, visit any branch for assisted registration.

Q: What is the daily transaction limit for NEFT/RTGS?
A: Transaction limits:
- NEFT: No minimum or maximum limit (RBI has removed limits)
- RTGS: Minimum Rs. 2 lakhs, maximum Rs. 10 lakhs per transaction
- IMPS: Maximum Rs. 5 lakhs per transaction
- UPI: Maximum Rs. 1 lakh per transaction (Rs. 2 lakhs for verified merchants)

Q: Are there charges for NEFT/RTGS transfers?
A: NEFT transactions are free of charge for online transfers. RTGS charges:
- Rs. 2 lakhs to Rs. 5 lakhs: Rs. 30 per transaction
- Above Rs. 5 lakhs: Rs. 55 per transaction
Branch-initiated NEFT/RTGS may have additional service charges.

SECTION 7: LOANS - GENERAL
----------------------------

Q: How is my EMI calculated?
A: EMI is calculated using the formula:
EMI = P × r × (1+r)^n / ((1+r)^n - 1)
Where:
P = Principal loan amount
r = Monthly interest rate (annual rate / 12)
n = Number of monthly installments

Q: What is the prepayment penalty on loans?
A: Prepayment charges:
- Home Loans (floating rate): Nil (as per RBI guidelines)
- Personal Loans: 2% to 5% of outstanding principal after 6 EMIs
- Car Loans: 2% to 5% of outstanding principal
- Business Loans: 1% to 3% of outstanding principal

Q: What happens if I miss an EMI?
A: If you miss an EMI:
1. A penal interest of 2% per month on the overdue amount is charged
2. Your credit score may be negatively impacted after 30 days of default
3. After 90 days of non-payment, the account may be classified as NPA
4. Legal action may be initiated for persistent defaults
Please contact us immediately if you are facing difficulty in repayment for restructuring options.

SECTION 8: BANKING TERMS AND DEFINITIONS
------------------------------------------

Q: What is a CIBIL score?
A: A CIBIL score (also called a Credit Score) is a 3-digit number ranging from 300 to 900 that represents your creditworthiness. It is generated by TransUnion CIBIL, India's leading credit bureau, based on your credit history. A higher score indicates better credit health:
- 750 to 900: Excellent - best loan rates, easy approval
- 700 to 749: Good - eligible for most loans
- 650 to 699: Fair - may face higher interest rates
- 600 to 649: Poor - loan approval difficult
- Below 600: Very Poor - likely to be rejected
Your CIBIL score is affected by timely EMI payments, credit card bill payments, loan defaults, number of active loans, and credit utilization ratio. You can check your CIBIL score for free once a year at www.cibil.com.

Q: What is KYC and why is it required?
A: KYC stands for Know Your Customer. It is a mandatory process required by RBI for all banks to verify the identity and address of their customers. KYC documents include Aadhaar Card, PAN Card, Passport, or Voter ID. KYC must be completed to open a bank account, apply for a loan, or invest in mutual funds.

Q: What is an EMI?
A: EMI stands for Equated Monthly Installment. It is a fixed payment amount made by a borrower to a lender on a specific date each month. EMI is composed of principal repayment and interest. The EMI amount depends on the loan amount, interest rate, and tenure.

Q: What is NPA?
A: NPA stands for Non-Performing Asset. A loan becomes NPA when the borrower fails to make repayments for 90 days or more. NPA classification negatively impacts the borrower's CIBIL score and may lead to legal recovery action by the bank.

Q: What is the repo rate?
A: The Repo Rate is the rate at which the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) lends money to commercial banks. When RBI increases the repo rate, home loan and other floating rate loan EMIs increase. When repo rate decreases, EMIs decrease.

Q: What is the difference between NEFT, RTGS and IMPS?
A: These are three modes of electronic fund transfer in India:
- NEFT (National Electronic Funds Transfer): Batch processing, available 24x7, no minimum or maximum limit, free for online transfers.
- RTGS (Real Time Gross Settlement): Real-time transfer, minimum Rs. 2 lakhs, used for large transfers, charges apply.
- IMPS (Immediate Payment Service): Instant transfer 24x7, maximum Rs. 5 lakhs, small charges may apply.

Q: What is net banking?
A: Net banking (Internet Banking) is an online banking service that allows customers to perform financial transactions through the bank website. You can check balances, transfer funds, pay bills, apply for loans, open FDs, and manage accounts from anywhere at any time.
